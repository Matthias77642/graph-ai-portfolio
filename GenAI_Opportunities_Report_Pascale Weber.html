Focus: Automating Student Administration Workflows

Executive Summary

The University of Zurich (UZH) stands at the forefront of AI integration in Switzerland, with established policies and executive education programs already in place. To address the specific challenge of student administration automation, this report proposes a transition from manual, high-volume administrative tasks to a GenAI-augmented ecosystem. By leveraging Large Language Models (LLMs) and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), UZH can significantly reduce response times, minimize manual data entry, and provide 24/7 personalized support to its diverse student body.

Company Overview

As Switzerland's largest university, UZH manages complex administrative workflows involving thousands of students, researchers, and staff. The institution's current commitment to "AI literacy" and its proactive approach to GenAI in research and teaching provide a strong cultural and technical foundation for administrative transformation. The focus on Swiss-based excellence requires solutions that are secure, multilingual, and highly accurate.

Institutional Strength

UZH already offers a CAS in Generative AI, indicating internal expertise that can be harnessed to champion and oversee administrative automation projects, ensuring they align with academic standards and data privacy laws.
